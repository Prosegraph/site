Prosegraph

Embeddable comment section with customizable content moderation for local newspaper websites.

The Challenge

One of the key problems medium-sized publishers face today is retaining reader engagement and loyalty for their digital offerings. Comment sections offered by the likes of OpenWeb and Disqus suffer from difficult customizability, high maintenance cost and low quality engagement, leading to many small publishers disabling comment sections.

Our Solution: Prosegraph

Prosegraph is a moderated comment section for any websites, designed to help small and medium-sized online publishers seamlessly integrate and maintain high-quality comment sections.

Features of Prosegraph

  • Bouncer: Automatically decides who can join communities based on criteria like email domain, subscription status, payment status.
  • Moderator: Keeps discussions appropriate and on-topic by specifying guidelines with natural language.
  • Identifier: Allows identity continuity across different publishers, establishing trust and increasing engagement.
  • Viewer: A light-weight UI that integrates seamlessly into articles and blogs for engaging discussions.

Success Story: The Amherst Student

The Amherst Student chose Prosegraph as its comment provider of choice for several reasons: highly automated and observable moderation policy, ease of gatekeeping with identifiers (filter by student ID), the lightweight viewer, and increased reader engagement.

Looking Ahead

We see the embeddable comment platform as a foundational part of our business model. In the future, Prosegraph aims to serve as a framework for building small online communities, offering an alternative to platforms like Discord, Reddit, web forums, and Facebook Groups.

Example Use Case

A community for NYU students searching for roommates could utilize Prosegraph's Bouncer for email verification (nyu.edu) and Moderator for focused discussions on roommate searching.

We are confident that Prosegraph can gain traction in the underserved web comment space, while simultaneously establishing a modular framework for building online communities.
